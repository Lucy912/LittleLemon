### [6:15-8:00] ADVANCED PROMPTS 8-10 (Guided - Higher Difficulty)

**PROMPT 8: Editing/Improving Existing Content**

**Scenario:** You wrote something but it's not quite right.

```
Act as an editor known for making content 50% better with minimal changes.

Task: Improve this [email/post/document] to be more [specific goal: persuasive/clear/engaging].

Context: Original version is [what's wrong: too long/too formal/confusing]. Audience is [who]. Goal is [desired action]. Constraints: Keep it under [word count], maintain [what to keep].

Format: Show before/after comparison with 3 bullet points explaining what changed and why.

Tone: In the revised version, make it [desired tone].

[Paste original content here]
```

**KEY LEARNING:** AI is excellent at editing when you tell it WHAT to improve and WHY.

---

**PROMPT 9: Brainstorming Multiple Options**

**Scenario:** You need creative ideas, not just one answer.

```
Act as a creative strategist who generates diverse, unconventional ideas.

Task: Brainstorm 10 different approaches to [your challenge].

Context: We've tried [what hasn't worked]. Constraints are [budget/time/resources]. Target audience is [who]. Success looks like [outcome]. We're open to unconventional ideas.

Format: Table with 4 columns: Idea Name, Description (1 sentence), Pros, Cons. Then rank top 3 with explanation.

Tone: Creative but practical, pushing boundaries while staying realistic.
```

**KEY LEARNING:** Ask for multiple options + ranking to get better decisions.

---

**PROMPT 10: Decision-Making Support**

**Scenario:** You're stuck between two choices and need clarity.

```
Act as a strategic advisor who helps people make difficult decisions with confidence.

Task: Help me decide between [Option A] and [Option B] for [situation].

Context: Option A = [description, pros, cons]. Option B = [description, pros, cons]. My constraints: [time/budget/risk tolerance]. My values: [what matters most]. Unknowns: [what I'm uncertain about].

Format:
1) Reframe the decision (is this the right question?)
2) Comparison table (criteria, A rating, B rating)
3) Risk analysis for each
4) Recommended choice with reasoning
5) What data would change this recommendation?

Tone: Objective and analytical, but acknowledging the emotional aspect of decisions.
```

**KEY LEARNING:** The more context about constraints and values, the better the decision support.

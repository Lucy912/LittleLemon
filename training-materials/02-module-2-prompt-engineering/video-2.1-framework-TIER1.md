### [2:30-6:00] THE 5-PART FRAMEWORK (Deep Dive)

**Part 1: ROLE** ğŸ­ (Who should AI be?)

**Why it matters:**
- AI adopts expertise and perspective
- Shapes the knowledge it draws from
- Influences language and depth

**Good examples:**
- "Act as a marketing strategist"
- "Act as a data analyst with 10 years experience"
- "Act as a teacher explaining to beginners"
- "Act as a CFO reviewing a proposal"

**Bad examples:**
- "You are AI" (too generic)
- No role specified (AI picks randomly)

**Quick Tip:** Match role to your needs. Technical task? Technical role. Creative task? Creative role.

---

**Part 2: TASK** ğŸ¯ (What exactly do you want?)

**Why it matters:**
- Defines the deliverable
- Sets clear boundaries
- Prevents scope creep

**Good examples:**
- "Write a 300-word product description"
- "Create a 5-step process for onboarding"
- "Analyze these 50 customer reviews and identify top 3 complaints"

**Bad examples:**
- "Help with product" (what kind of help?)
- "Do something with reviews" (what output?)

**Quick Tip:** Use action verbs: Write, Create, Analyze, Summarize, List, Compare, Design, Plan

---

**Part 3: CONTEXT** ğŸ“‹ (Why does this matter?)

**Why it matters:**
- Provides background AI can't guess
- Influences relevance of output
- Enables nuanced responses

**Good examples:**
- "Context: This is for a B2B SaaS company selling to CFOs. Our main differentiator is security."
- "Context: I'm presenting to a skeptical board who thinks AI is overhyped. I need data-driven arguments."
- "Context: Budget is tight ($500), timeline is urgent (2 weeks), team is non-technical."

**Bad examples:**
- No context (AI makes assumptions)
- Too much context (irrelevant details)

**Quick Tip:** Answer: Who's this for? What constraints exist? What's the goal?

---

**Part 4: FORMAT** ğŸ“ (How should it look?)

**Why it matters:**
- Defines structure and organization
- Makes output immediately usable
- Prevents reformatting work

**Good examples:**
- "Format: Table with 3 columns: Feature, Benefit, Pricing"
- "Format: Executive summary (3 sentences), then 5 bullet points, then recommendation"
- "Format: Email with subject line, greeting, 3 paragraphs, call-to-action, sign-off"

**Bad examples:**
- "Make it look good" (subjective)
- No format specified (AI picks randomly)

**Quick Tip:** Be specific: table, list, paragraph, email, report, code, JSON, etc.

---

**Part 5: TONE** ğŸ¨ (How should it sound?)

**Why it matters:**
- Matches your brand/voice
- Sets appropriate formality
- Influences emotional resonance

**Good examples:**
- "Tone: Conversational but professional, warm and encouraging"
- "Tone: Direct and data-driven, confident but not arrogant"
- "Tone: Friendly and casual, like talking to a friend over coffee"
- "Tone: Formal and authoritative, appropriate for legal context"

**Bad examples:**
- "Sound good" (subjective)
- "Be nice" (vague)
- No tone (AI defaults to neutral/boring)

**Quick Tip:** Describe it like you would to a writer: professional, casual, witty, serious, warm, direct

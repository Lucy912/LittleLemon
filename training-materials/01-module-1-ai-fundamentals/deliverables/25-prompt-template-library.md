# AI Prompt Template Library
## 25 Ready-to-Use Prompts Using the 5-Part Framework

**Created by:** Lucy's AI Training
**Framework:** Role → Task → Context → Format → Tone
**How to use:** Copy any prompt, customize the bracketed [sections] for your needs, paste into ChatGPT/Claude

## Category 1: Business Communication (4 prompts)

### 1.1 Professional Email - Declining Request

```
Act as a busy professional who values relationships.

Task: Write an email declining [type of request: meeting/project/partnership].

Context: The request is from [relationship: vendor/colleague/potential client]. I'm declining because [reason: budget constraints/time/not a fit], but I want to [goal: maintain good relations/leave door open/refer them elsewhere].

Format: Professional email, 3-4 sentences maximum.

Tone: Warm but direct, apologetic but not over-explaining.
```

**Customization tips:** Replace [type of request], [relationship], [reason], and [goal] with your specifics.

---

### 1.2 Meeting Agenda Creator

```
Act as an executive assistant skilled at running efficient meetings.

Task: Create a meeting agenda for [meeting purpose: project kickoff/quarterly review/problem-solving session].

Context: Meeting length is [duration], attendees are [roles/departments], and the main goal is to [objective: align on strategy/make a decision/solve a specific problem].

Format: Structured agenda with time allocations, bullet points for each topic, clear action items.

Tone: Professional and organized, focused on outcomes.
```

**Use case:** Any meeting where you want to maximize efficiency and clarity.

---

### 1.3 Difficult Conversation Script

```
Act as a communication coach specializing in workplace conflict resolution.

Task: Help me prepare for a difficult conversation about [topic: performance issue/project delay/budget cut/role change].

Context: I need to discuss this with [person: direct report/manager/client]. The challenge is [specific difficulty: they're defensive/I'm nervous/topic is sensitive]. My goal is [desired outcome: behavior change/reset expectations/find compromise].

Format: Opening statement, 3 key points to address, suggested responses to common objections, closing statement.

Tone: Direct but empathetic, firm but respectful, solution-focused.
```

**Pro tip:** Practice this out loud before the actual conversation.

---

### 1.4 Status Update / Progress Report

```
Act as a project manager who communicates clearly and proactively.

Task: Write a status update for [project/initiative name] covering the past [week/month/quarter].

Context: Audience is [stakeholders: executive team/client/cross-functional team]. Key achievements are [list 2-3], current challenges are [list 1-2], and upcoming priorities are [list 2-3].

Format: Executive summary (2 sentences), bullet-point sections for Achievements, Challenges, Next Steps, each with 2-4 items.

Tone: Confident, transparent about challenges, forward-looking.
```

**Time saved:** Turns 30-minute task into 5 minutes.

## Category 2: Data Analysis & Decision-Making (3 prompts)

### 2.1 Customer Feedback Analyzer

```
Act as a customer insights analyst.

Task: Analyze this customer feedback to identify the top [3-5] pain points and recommend [2-3] actionable solutions for each.

Context: This feedback is from [source: surveys/support tickets/reviews] about [product/service/process]. Current [metric: satisfaction score/completion rate] is [X]%, and we want to increase it to [Y]%. We have [resource constraints: limited budget/small team/technical limitations].

Format: Create a table with columns: Pain Point, Impact (High/Medium/Low), Recommended Solution, Estimated Effort (Low/Medium/High). Then add a brief summary paragraph with top priority.

Tone: Data-driven but actionable, focus on quick wins.
```

**Paste your data after this prompt.** Can handle 500+ feedback entries.

---

### 2.2 Decision Framework Builder

```
Act as a strategic advisor helping leaders make complex decisions.

Task: Create a decision-making framework for [decision: which vendor to choose/whether to launch product/how to prioritize projects].

Context: The key factors to consider are [list 3-5 factors: cost, timeline, risk, impact, feasibility]. Stakeholders include [who's involved]. Decision must be made by [deadline].

Format: Decision matrix with weighted criteria (assign % weight to each factor), evaluation scale (1-5), and space for notes on each option.

Tone: Objective and analytical, but practical.
```

**Use for:** Any decision with multiple options and competing priorities.

### 2.3 Performance Data Summarizer

```
Act as a data analyst who creates executive-friendly reports.

Task: Summarize this [sales/marketing/operations/financial] data and highlight the most important trends and insights.

Context: This data covers [time period], the audience is [executives/team leads/board members] who need to understand [key questions: what's working, what's not, where to focus]. They have [time constraint: 5 minutes/10 minutes] to review.

Format: 3-5 bullet point key insights, each with supporting data point. Include 1-2 recommended actions. Use simple language, avoid jargon.

Tone: Clear and direct, highlight what matters most.
```

**Paste data below prompt.** Works with tables, CSV, or text summaries.

## Category 3: Content Creation (4 prompts)

### 3.1 LinkedIn Post Generator

```
Act as a thought leader in [your industry/expertise area] with a conversational, authentic style.

Task: Write a LinkedIn post about [topic].

Context: My goal is to [educate/inspire/challenge/share experience]. My audience is [job titles/industries] who care about [pain point/interest]. I want to position myself as [your unique angle].

Format: Hook in first line (under 10 words), 3-4 short paragraphs (2-3 sentences each), end with a question or call-to-action. Use line breaks for readability.

Tone: [Choose: conversational but professional/bold and contrarian/warm and encouraging/data-driven and analytical].
```

**Variation:** Replace LinkedIn with Twitter/X, Instagram, or blog post and adjust format.

---

### 3.2 Blog Post Outliner

```
Act as a content strategist who creates engaging, SEO-friendly content.

Task: Create a detailed outline for a blog post titled "[your title]" or about [topic].

Context: Target audience is [specific reader], their main question is "[question they're trying to answer]", and they're at [awareness stage: beginner/intermediate/advanced]. Post goal is to [educate/convert/build trust]. Target length is [800-1200/1500-2000/2500+] words.

Format: H1 title, introduction (hook + promise), 5-7 H2 sections with 2-4 H3 sub-points each, conclusion with clear CTA. Include suggestions for examples, data points, or stories to include.

Tone: [Match your brand voice: friendly and accessible/authoritative and expert/casual and fun].
```

**Next step:** Use this outline with another prompt to write each section.

### 3.3 Social Media Content Calendar

```
Act as a social media manager who plans strategic, engaging content.

Task: Create a [1-week/2-week/month] content calendar for [platform: LinkedIn/Instagram/Twitter/TikTok].

Context: My business/brand is [description], target audience is [specific demographics/psychographics], and my goals are [awareness/engagement/leads/sales]. I can commit to posting [X times per week]. Content pillars are [3-5 themes you cover].

Format: Calendar table with columns: Date, Post Type (educational/inspirational/promotional/engagement), Topic, Hook/Caption Idea, CTA.

Tone: Strategic but realistic, varied content mix, aligned with brand voice.

Act as an email marketer who writes newsletters people actually want to read.

## Category 5: Learning & Research (3 prompts)

### 5.1 "Explain It Like I'm 5" (ELI5)

```
Act as a teacher who explains complex topics in simple, relatable terms.

Task: Explain [complex topic/concept] in a way that someone with no background in [field] can understand.

Context: I need to [understand this myself/explain to non-technical stakeholder/teach a beginner]. The most confusing parts are [specific concepts]. Use analogies and examples from [everyday life/business/sports/cooking].

Format: Start with a simple one-sentence definition, then 2-3 paragraph explanation using analogies, end with a concrete example.

Tone: Patient and encouraging, avoid jargon, use vivid analogies.
```

**Perfect for:** Understanding AI, tech, finance, science, or any complex topic.

Format: Executive summary (2-3 sentences), key findings (5-7 bullet points), implications for [my context], recommended actions or further reading.

Tone: Objective and analytical, highlight what's most useful.
```

## Category 7: Sales & Marketing (3 prompts)

### 7.1 Value Proposition Crafter

```
Act as a messaging strategist who creates compelling value propositions.

Task: Write a clear value proposition for [product/service/offer].

Context: Target customer is [specific persona/industry]. Their main problem is [pain point], current alternatives are [competitor solutions/status quo], and our unique advantage is [differentiator]. They care most about [value driver: time/money/quality/ease/results].

Format: One-sentence value prop, followed by 3 supporting benefit statements. Include a before/after comparison.

Tone: Customer-centric (focus on their outcome, not our features), clear and specific.
```

**This becomes:** Your homepage headline, pitch deck opening, sales email hook.

### 7.2 Sales Email Sequence

```
Act as a sales professional who writes emails that get responses.

Context: Prospect is [title/industry], they care about [pain points/goals], we've [previous interaction or research finding]. Goal of sequence is [book meeting/get response/close deal/re-engage].

### 7.3 Objection Handler

```
Act as a sales coach who helps reps overcome objections with confidence.

Task: Provide effective responses to the objection: "[specific objection: too expensive/need to think about it/not the right time/using competitor/need approval]."

Context: This objection typically comes up [when: during discovery/at proposal stage/after demo]. The underlying concern is usually [real reason: budget/risk/priorities/trust]. I want to [address concern/reframe/ask questions to understand better].

## How to Get Even Better Results

### Iteration Tips:
1. **Start with the template** as-is to see baseline results
2. **Customize** the bracketed sections with your specific details
3. **Add examples** if you have them ("similar to this: [paste example]")
4. **Refine the output** by saying "Make it more [specific quality]" or "Change [section] to focus on [different angle]"
5. **Save your best versions** - build your own personalized prompt library

### Framework Reminder:
Every prompt here follows the 5-Part Framework:
- **Role** - Who the AI should be
- **Task** - What you want done
- **Context** - Why it matters, background details
- **Format** - How it should be structured
- **Tone** - The voice and style

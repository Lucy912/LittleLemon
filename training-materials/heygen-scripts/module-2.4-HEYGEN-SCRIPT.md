## SCENE 3: Marketing & Content Prompts (1:00-2:00)
**Slide:** Marketing category with 8 prompt types listed

Category one: marketing and content. Eight prompts covering the most common marketing tasks.

Content strategy prompt. "Act as a content strategist with B2B expertise. Task: create a ninety-day content plan for your platform. Context: target audience, pain points, differentiation, goal. Format: table with week, topic, content type, keyword, CTA, success metric. Tone: strategic and practical, focused on conversion not vanity metrics."

Social media post prompt. "Act as a social media manager known for high engagement. Task: write a LinkedIn or Twitter post. Context: what happened and why it matters, audience, goal. Format: hook, story or insight, key takeaway, question to prompt comments. Tone: conversational but professional, human not corporate."

Email marketing campaign. "Act as an email marketing specialist. Task: write a five-email nurture sequence. Context: goal, main objection, key differentiator. Format: for each email, subject line, preview text, body, CTA, then explain the sequence strategy. Tone: helpful not salesy, addressing objections, building trust through value."

Three more quickly. Blog post outline for SEO. Ad copy for Google or Facebook with character limits. Landing page copy focused on conversion. Case study interview questions. Content repurposing, turning one blog into five social posts. All in your downloadable library.

## SCENE 4: Sales & Customer Success Prompts (2:00-3:00)
**Slide:** Sales category with prompt examples

Category two: sales and customer success. Eight prompts for the full sales cycle.

Cold outreach email. "Act as a B2B sales professional with high response rates. Task: write cold outreach to job title at company type. Context: personalization hook, their likely pain, what you're asking for. Format: subject line, three short paragraphs, clear ask, P.S. with social proof. Tone: respectful of time, focused on their problem not our product."

Demo prep and discovery. "Act as a sales engineer who runs consultative demos. Task: create demo prep guide. Context: company details, current solution, stated pain, decision maker, budget, timeline. Format: five sections covering research, discovery questions, demo flow, objections, closing questions. Tone: consultative not salesy, focused on outcomes not features."

Follow-up after no response. "Act as persistent but respectful sales professional. Task: write follow-up after no response. Context: last contact timeframe, what you discussed, why they might be silent. Format: brief reminder, value add, low-pressure re-ask. Tone: understanding and helpful, providing new value not just checking in."

Handling price objections. Customer success check-ins for low usage accounts. Win-loss interview questions. Contract negotiation talking points. Upsell opportunities based on usage patterns. All structured using the framework.
